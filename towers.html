Tower Bureau
A latent signal map

Most of the time the city watches us through things we cannot see. While you move through the streets, your phone continuously negotiates with the base stations and antennas around you — each handshake leaves a record of where you were, when, and for how long.

The Tower Bureau makes this radio infrastructure visible. It collects the coordinates of cell towers from the OpenCelliD open database — the largest community map of the world's cells — and renders the latent signal topology of your surroundings: the towers, their estimated coverage, and the constant pulse of the network that registers every passing device.

The map is drawn live on your machine with raw WebGL shaders. Nothing about you is stored or sent anywhere except the single query to OpenCelliD for the towers near your position.

Get a free key at opencellid.org, or run the bureau on synthetic towers.
